Campaigns are automated outreach sequences you use to recruit new advocates, collect content, broadcast information, or run AI-powered interviews — all from the **Campaigns** section in Deeto.

## Overview

Deeto has five campaign types. Each one serves a different goal, targets a different audience, and tracks different results. When you create a new campaign, you'll be asked to pick one of these types.

| Campaign type | What it's for | Audience | Key metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New Advocates** | Recruit new customers as references | External contacts (not yet in Deeto) | # of new customers added |
| **Existing Advocates** | Collect content from current references | References already in your account | # of new assets created |
| **Broadcast Information** | Send a message to all your references | All current references | Audience size reached |
| **AI Interview** | Run AI-powered interviews with references | References already in your account | # of new customers added |
| **Potential Customers** | Generate content from prospects | Prospects / potential customers | # of new assets created |

---

## New Advocates

**What it does:** Sends an automated email sequence to people who aren't yet in Deeto, inviting them to join as customer references. When someone signs up, they become a new customer contact in your account.

**Use this when:** You want to grow your pool of customer advocates and have a list of customers to invite.

**What Deeto tracks:**
- Number of new customers added through this campaign
- Conversion rate (how many invitees became advocates)

**How it works:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define the audience">
    Upload contacts or add them manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure an email sequence">
    Set up one or more emails, spaced by days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch">
    Deeto sends the emails automatically and registers anyone who signs up as a new advocate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Existing Advocates

**What it does:** Asks your current references to create content — like testimonials, case studies, or reviews — through an automated email sequence that links them to Deeto's contribution wizard.

**Use this when:** You have existing references and want to collect fresh content from them.

**What Deeto tracks:**
- Number of new content assets created
- Percentage of the audience who contributed

**How it works:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select an audience">
    Choose from your existing references manually or by segment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up the email sequence">
    Include a link to the contribution experience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="References contribute">
    References click the link, complete the contribution wizard, and submit content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Broadcast Information

**What it does:** Sends a single message or announcement to your references at once. Unlike other campaign types, there's no follow-up sequence — it's a one-time broadcast.

**Use this when:** You want to share news, a product update, or an invitation with your entire reference base.

**What Deeto tracks:**
- Total audience size (how many references received the broadcast)
- Percentage who engaged

<Note>
  Broadcast is a one-time send — there's no multi-step follow-up sequence.
</Note>

---

## AI Interview

**What it does:** Uses Deeto's AI agent to conduct structured interviews with your references. The AI handles the conversation automatically — asking questions, following up, and capturing responses — without you needing to be present.

**Use this when:** You want to collect detailed, interview-style feedback or stories from references at scale, without scheduling calls.

**What Deeto tracks:**
- Number of new customers added through this campaign
- Conversion rate

**Topic scopes available:**

- Win / Loss analysis
- New product feedback
- First 30 days experience
- Pre-renewal
- NPS / follow-up feedback
- Retention analysis
- Product-specific feedback

---

## Potential Customers

**What it does:** Generates content assets from people who are prospects — not yet customers. Designed for outreach to potential customers and capturing their perspectives for content.

**Use this when:** You want to generate content from a prospect audience rather than existing references.

**What Deeto tracks:**
- Number of new assets created
- Percentage of the audience who contributed

## Campaign statuses

Regardless of type, every campaign moves through these statuses:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Created but not yet launched |
| **Scheduled** | Set to launch at a future date/time |
| **Active** | Running — emails are going out |
| **Stopped** | Paused manually |
| **Ended** | Reached its completion condition automatically |
| **Archived** | Closed out and no longer shown by default |

---

## Things to keep in mind

- Filter the campaign list by type using the **Campaign type** filter at the top of the Campaigns page.
- You can also filter by status to see only active, draft, or ended campaigns.
- Once a campaign is active, recipients who unsubscribe are automatically removed and won't receive further emails.
- If a campaign's completion condition is met (e.g. a target number of responses), it ends automatically.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I change the campaign type after creating it?">
    No — the type is set when you create the campaign and can't be changed afterward.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between New Advocates and AI Interview?">
    Both recruit new customers, but New Advocates uses a standard email sequence that you write, while AI Interview uses Deeto's AI agent to conduct a structured conversation. AI Interview is better for collecting rich, detailed stories — New Advocates is better for straightforward invitations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between Existing Advocates and Broadcast Information?">
    Existing Advocates is designed to collect content (testimonials, reviews) and links to Deeto's contribution wizard. Broadcast Information is just for sending a message — it doesn't collect any content.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
