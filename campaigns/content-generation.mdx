A successful content generation campaign doesn't just collect feedback — it uncovers the most compelling stories from your customers. Here's how to build a campaign that captures high-quality contributions at scale.

## 1. Segment your audience

To make sure your campaign resonates, segment your audience based on:

- Industry or company size
- Role (e.g., decision-makers vs. daily users)
- Stage in the customer journey
- Product line

By narrowing your focus, you can ask more targeted, relevant questions and collect more meaningful insights.

## 2. Choose a topic that matters

Select a prompt that reflects a common success point, challenge, or outcome your customers care about. Great examples include:

- "How has [feature] transformed your workflow?"
- "Tell us about a win your team achieved using [product]"

## 3. Emphasize rewards

Incentives drive action. Clearly communicate what contributors will receive and why it's worth their time. Mention the reward in your:

- Subject line
- Campaign intro
- Follow-up emails

## 4. Write a follow-up email sequence

Don't rely on a single email to drive responses. Plan a series of at least **4 follow-ups**:

- A reminder for those who haven't opened
- A last-chance message with urgency

<Tip>Send emails from a familiar name or domain to improve open rates.</Tip>

## 5. Keep it simple

Deeto's flows are designed to be user-friendly. Keep your questions clear, and:

- Focus on the relevant topic
- Provide examples when possible
- Let users pause and return later

## 6. Review and reuse

Once submissions come in, use them across:

- Sales collateral
- Customer [microsites](/microsites/overview)
- Marketing materials
- [Website widgets](/publishing/website-widget)
