AI Interview campaigns use Deeto's AI agent to conduct structured interviews with your references. The AI handles the conversation automatically — asking questions, following up, and capturing responses — without you needing to be present.

## When to use AI Interviews

- You want **detailed, interview-style feedback** at scale
- You need to understand the **"why" behind patterns** in your data
- You want to fill **data gaps** identified by [Research Hub](/research/best-practices) analysis
- You need **consistent data collection** across many references

## Topic scopes

When creating an AI Interview campaign, choose a topic that focuses the conversation:

| Topic scope | Best for | Example research question |
|---|---|---|
| **Win/Loss analysis** | Understanding deal outcomes | "Why did the customer choose us over [competitor]?" |
| **Loss analysis** | Deep dive into lost deals | "What were the key factors in choosing another solution?" |
| **New product feedback** | Post-launch feedback | "How has [feature] changed your daily workflow?" |
| **First 30 days experience** | Onboarding quality | "What surprised you most about implementation?" |
| **Pre-renewal** | Retention signals | "What would make you hesitate to renew?" |
| **NPS / follow-up feedback** | Score context | "You gave us a 7 — what would make it a 9?" |
| **Retention analysis** | Churn prevention | "What keeps you using the platform daily?" |
| **Product-specific feedback** | Feature deep-dive | "Walk me through how you use [feature]" |

## Setting up an AI Interview campaign

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the campaign">
    Go to **Workflows → Campaigns → Create Campaign** and select **AI Interview Agent**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the topic scope">
    Select one of the topic scopes above. This determines the AI's conversation direction and follow-up questions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define your audience">
    Select which references to interview. For best results, target a specific segment rather than your entire base.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the email invitation">
    Write the email that invites references to participate. Mention the estimated time (typically 10-15 minutes) and any rewards.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch">
    Activate the campaign. References receive the invitation and can start their AI interview at any time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How the AI interview works (reference perspective)

1. Reference clicks the interview link from the email
2. AI greets them and explains the purpose
3. AI asks open-ended questions based on the chosen topic
4. AI follows up based on responses — probing deeper where interesting signals appear
5. Interview is automatically transcribed and summarized
6. Transcript and summary appear in your [Knowledge Hub](/knowledge-hub/overview)

## What you get from AI Interviews

| Output | Where to find it |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Full transcript** | Knowledge Hub → Content → Transcripts |
| **AI summary** | Attached to the transcript as a summary card |
| **Key quotes** | Automatically extracted and tagged |
| **Sentiment analysis** | Tagged on the transcript content object |

## Best practices

- **Start small** — Run your first AI interview with 10-15 references to validate the experience
- **Be specific with topic scope** — "Product-specific feedback" with a named feature produces better results than "general"
- **Time it right** — Post-implementation (30-60 days) and pre-renewal are the highest-signal moments
- **Combine with Research Hub** — Use AI Interview results to fill gaps identified by [research projects](/research/best-practices)
- **Offer incentives** — Reward participation to improve response rates

<Tip>Launch a targeted AI Interview campaign at the midpoint of a Research Hub project. The first-half findings tell you exactly what questions to ask.</Tip>
