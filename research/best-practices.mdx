## 1. Setting up your first research project

Your first research project sets the foundation for your customer intelligence program. Choose a project that has clear stakeholder demand and enough existing data.

**Pre-launch checklist:**

- [ ] Confirm Research Hub is activated and accessible
- [ ] Verify you have 50+ VoC assets connected (interviews, surveys, reviews, feedback)
- [ ] Identify your first research question (see project templates below)
- [ ] Confirm at least one stakeholder who will consume the results
- [ ] Set a timeline for your first findings review (3 weeks recommended)

**Recommended first projects:**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Win-Loss Analysis">
    Best first project if you have deal outcome data. Immediate value for sales and product marketing.

    **Research question:** "Why are we winning and losing deals, and what patterns emerge across segments?"
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Product Feedback Research">
    Best first project if you have strong product usage data. Immediate value for product management.

    **Research question:** "What are our customers' top pain points and feature requests, and how do they vary by segment?"
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Customer Health Tracking">
    Best first project if you have broad feedback data. Immediate value for CS leadership.

    **Research question:** "What's the overall sentiment trend across our customer base, and which accounts show risk signals?"
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>Start with a project where you already have a hypothesis. This lets you validate the Research Hub's findings against your intuition, building trust in the platform.</Tip>

## 2. Designing effective research projects

Well-scoped research projects produce actionable findings. Poorly scoped ones produce interesting data nobody uses.

| Element | What to define | Example |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| Research question | One clear question you want answered | "Why are enterprise deals taking 30% longer to close this quarter?" |
| Data sources | Which VoC data to include | Win-loss interviews, sales call recordings, CRM notes |
| Time frame | What period to analyze | Last 2 quarters, with trend comparison to prior year |
| Segments | How to slice the analysis | By deal size, industry, competitor mentioned |
| Stakeholders | Who will consume findings | VP Sales, Head of Product, CRO |
| Decision context | What decision this research will inform | Whether to restructure the enterprise sales process |

<Warning>Common mistake: Trying to answer too many questions in one project. Start narrow. You can always expand scope or launch a follow-up project.</Warning>

## 3. Using AI Interviews within research

Research Hub includes AI Interview capabilities. Use them to fill data gaps and collect targeted qualitative data.

**When to add AI Interviews to a project:**

- Your research project identifies a pattern but you need deeper "why" data
- You have a hypothesis from existing data that needs validation with a larger sample
- A specific segment is underrepresented in your VoC data
- You want to test a new research question before committing to a full project

| Practice | Why it matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Design interview questions from research findings | Ensures you're asking about what the data is already telling you |
| Target interviews to fill specific gaps | Don't interview broadly; interview the segments where you need more signal |
| Review interview results within the research project | Keep all insights connected rather than analyzing interviews in isolation |
| Use interview quotes to enrich research findings | Stakeholders respond to specific customer voices, not just aggregate patterns |

<Tip>Launch a targeted AI Interview campaign at the midpoint of a research project. The first-half findings will tell you exactly what questions to ask.</Tip>

## 4. Presenting findings to stakeholders

Research only creates value when stakeholders act on it. Tailor your delivery to each audience.

| Audience | Format | Content focus | Timing |
|----------|--------|--------------|--------|
| C-Suite / Board | 1-page executive summary | Top 3 findings + strategic implications | Quarterly or at milestone |
| Product Leadership | Prioritized insights report | Feature requests ranked by frequency and urgency | Monthly or per project |
| Sales Leadership | Battle card updates + competitive brief | Win-loss patterns, competitive positioning shifts | Monthly or per project |
| CS Leadership | Account health dashboard + risk alerts | Sentiment trends, at-risk accounts, expansion signals | Bi-weekly or continuous |
| Marketing | Messaging validation + competitive intel | How customers describe your product | Per project or quarterly |

<Note>The single most important thing for Research Hub adoption is closing the loop. When stakeholders see that research leads to decisions, they request more research.</Note>

## 5. Building a research cadence

Move from one-off projects to a recurring research program that drives continuous intelligence.

| Project type | Frequency | Triggers |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Win-Loss Analysis | Continuous (rolling) | Always running; review findings monthly |
| Customer Health Tracking | Continuous (rolling) | Always running; review trends bi-weekly |
| Product Feedback Research | Quarterly | Aligned with product planning cycles |
| Competitive Intelligence | Quarterly or on-demand | Triggered by competitive moves or pipeline shifts |
| Annual Customer Intelligence Report | Annually | Aligned with annual planning or board meeting |

## 6. Measuring success

| Metric | Target | How to track |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Active research projects | 2+ running simultaneously | Research project dashboard |
| Time to first findings | Under 3 weeks from project start | Project timeline |
| Stakeholder reach | Insights shared with 3+ departments | Distribution tracking |
| Decisions influenced | 1+ strategic decision per quarter | Stakeholder follow-up |
| Inbound requests | Stakeholders requesting new research | Request log / CSM notes |

## 7. Troubleshooting

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Findings seem surface-level | Check data volume and diversity. Most projects need 100+ data points. Consider launching AI Interviews to add qualitative depth. |
| Stakeholders aren't engaging | You may be delivering in the wrong format. Ask each stakeholder what format they prefer. Lead with the "so what" — not the data. |
| Not sure what to research next | Ask your stakeholders: "What's the biggest customer-related question you can't answer today?" |
| Too many projects, not enough depth | Consolidate. Better to run 2 rich projects than 5 thin ones. |
| Data from different sources is conflicting | This is actually a finding. Slice by segment to find the story. |
